Wasatch Mountain Range

While Utah has many mountains, 85% of Utah's residents live within 15 miles of the Wasatch mountain range. With a total of seven canyons feeding into the Salt Lake Valley, the Wasatch mountains are a defining characteristic of Utah's environment and personality. The mountains are a vital source of water and provide many natural resources such as timber and granite.

City Creek Center

City Creek Center is a multi-use complex that is located in the heart of downtown Salt Lake City. City Creek houses many attractions and utilities. Everything from shopping centers to indoor dining. The area also holds more practical purposes in the form of offices and residential housing. With an artificial stream that runs through the building's superstructure, City Creek is a common tourist destination.

Natural History Museum

With over 5,000 artifacts on display, the Natural history Museum of Utah is one of the finest museums in the country. The museum manages exhibits on paleotonology, zoology, botany, and many other subjects. Located at the base of the mountains, the building has more than 1.6 million objects and can be used by scholars and the public alike.

Liberty Park

Liberty Park is the oldest and second largest public park in Salt Lake City. At 80 acres, the park houses four volleyball courts, one basketball court, three bocce ball courts, 10 horshoe courts, a swimming pool, a jogging path, a gazebo for reservation, and many other amenities. Liberty park is also the location of the oldest commercial building standing in Utah, the Isaac Chase Mill.

State Capitol

The State capitol building is located on a hill overlooking downtown Salt lake City. Inside, the capitol holds the House of Representatives and the state Supreme Court, a room called the Hall of Govenors with paintings of all of Utah's previous govenors, the main floor, which sits directly underneath the building's rotunda, and many other rooms. Tours are available from 10 AM to 3 PM Monday-Friday.
